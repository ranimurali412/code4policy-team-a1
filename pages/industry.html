Racial and gender gaps in the climate industry

Sex segregation is a key part of the US labor market.

Women are most likely employed in services and administrative work, and least likely to be working in construction and production industries.

Sex segregation is pronounced in climate jobs

Racial disparities are also a key part of the US labor market.

White individuals are overrepresented in natural resources sectors, while Black and Hispanic or Latino individuals are overrepresented in services, occupations that are likely lower paid.

Racial disparities are pronounced in climate jobs
